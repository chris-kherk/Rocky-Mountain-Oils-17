Manipura: The Solar Plexus Chakra or Third Chakra

The solar plexus chakra is located along the spine at the solar plexus, in the upper abdomen approximately two inches above the navel. It’s the third of the seven chakras and is also referred to by its Sanskrit name, Manipura, which translates to “shining gem.” Occasionally, it is referred to as the yellow chakra.

Personal power is the core of the solar plexus chakra. It represents authenticity, confidence, self-responsibility, and self-discipline. When properly aligned, the solar plexus chakra allows us to listen to our intuition more clearly, set healthy boundaries, and stand in our power. It lets our most authentic self shine brightly. The solar plexus chakra also embodies unlimited potential to transform thought or inertia into action.

A balanced solar plexus chakra gives you the ability to overcome fear, master your thoughts, and make conscious choices. The solar plexus chakra helps grow your confidence and vitality. This empowers you to follow your true path and create the life you want. When balanced, the solar plexus chakra allows you to seize your personal power, develop your authentic self, and take responsibility for your life.

Key Characteristics of the Solar Plexus Chakra

  • Identity: As the core of your personality and identity, the solar plexus chakra allows you to bloom into your authentic self and discover who you truly are.
  • Personal Power: The solar plexus chakra is the source of your personal power. This empowers you to take control of your thoughts, emotions, and actions.
  • Willpower: Channeling this personal power, your solar plexus chakra gives you willpower and self-discipline.
  • Confidence: The solar plexus chakra is responsible for your self-esteem and how you view yourself. It also gives you the confidence to make decisions and feel self-assured.
  • Intention & Action: Your solar plexus chakra gives you the power to transform your thought into action.

How to Unblock Solar Plexus Chakra (3rd Chakra)

Conflict, stress, emotional distress, and illness can all cause misalignments or blockages within the chakra system. When the solar plexus chakra becomes unbalanced or blocked, it can bring a lack of control in your life as well as a loss of identity. If you’re working towards improving your self-esteem, moving into a leadership position at work, or trying to create more independence and self-sufficiency in your life, it’s vital to open and balance your solar plexus chakra.

To do this, you must first understand whether your solar plexus chakra is blocked (underactive) or too open (overactive). Depending on what your chakra needs, you many benefit from solar plexus chakra affirmations, meditation, mantra, yoga, or other activities.

Underactive Solar Plexus Chakra

When your solar plexus chakra is underactive, you may be experiencing a sense of a lack of control and purpose in life. A blocked solar plexus chakra often leads to emotional problems, ranging from feeling powerless to self-doubting. You may also have difficulty setting boundaries, making decisions, and listening to your gut intuition.

How to Recognize If Your Solar Plexus Chakra is Blocked

Your solar plexus or third chakra may be blocked if you are experiencing…

  • Lack of confidence
  • Low self-esteem
  • Indecisiveness
  • Helplessness
  • Lack of purpose
  • Difficulty motivating yourself

Overactive Solar Plexus Chakra

When your solar plexus chakra is overactive, you may find yourself judging too harshly, desiring to control others, or blaming others without taking personal responsibility for your actions. You may feel especially critical and stubborn. Someone with an overactive solar plexus chakra may have difficulty receiving feedback and admitting they’re wrong.

How to Recognize if Your Solar Plexus Chakra is Too Open

Your solar plexus or third chakra may be too open if you feel…

  • A desire to control others
  • Overly critical or judgmental
  • Stubbornness; refusal to compromise
  • Anger and aggression
  • Shifting blame onto other people
